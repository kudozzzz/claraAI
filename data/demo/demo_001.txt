Sarah: Hi Mike, thanks for joining today's demo call. I'm Sarah from Clara Answers. We help service trade businesses handle inbound calls with an AI voice agent. How are you doing today?

Mike: Good, good. Thanks for reaching out. We've been having a real problem with after-hours calls. Our guys are getting woken up at 2 AM for things that aren't really emergencies.

Sarah: That's exactly the problem we solve. Can you tell me a bit about your business first?

Mike: Sure. We're ProFire Solutions, we do fire protection work — sprinkler systems, fire suppression, alarm monitoring. We're based out of Denver, Colorado. Got about 30 field techs.

Sarah: Great. And when do you typically get these after-hours calls?

Mike: Mostly nights and weekends. We're open Monday through Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM Mountain Time. But after hours, our on-call tech gets bombarded. Sarah: So you want the AI to differentiate between true emergencies and routine requests?

Mike: Exactly. A true emergency for us is an active sprinkler discharge, a fire suppression system discharge, or a fire alarm that's actively going off. Those get routed to our on-call immediately. Everything else can wait.

Sarah: Got it. And who's the on-call person they'd be transferred to?

Mike: We have a rotating on-call schedule. Whoever is on-call that week gets the call. There's usually a primary and a backup.

Sarah: Makes sense. Let me show you how Clara handles this...

Sarah: So as you saw, Clara greeted the caller, asked why they were calling, determined it was an emergency, collected their name, callback number, and site address, then transferred to your on-call.
